# Checkpoint Answer Key

## Answers

- The dot product behaves like a weighted combination because each feature is multiplied by a weight and the results are summed.
- The derivative of `x^2` is larger at `x = 2` than at `x = 1` because the slope grows as `x` moves away from zero.
- Sample variance measures spread, while sample mean measures central tendency.
- Cross-entropy grows when confident predictions are wrong because the predicted probability assigned to the true outcome becomes very small.
- In the `x^2` example, moving `x` toward zero lowers the loss because the square gets smaller as the magnitude of `x` shrinks.
